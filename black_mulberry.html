Black Mulberry

Black mulberry (Morus nigra) is a long-lived fruit tree known for its rich, dark berries and sweet-tart flavor. It has been cultivated for thousands of years in the Middle East and Mediterranean regions.

Description

Black mulberries are large, dark purple to black aggregate fruits made up of many small drupelets. They are juicy, soft, and stain easily due to their deep pigment.

Distribution and Habitat

Black mulberry is found in:

  • Middle East
  • Southern Europe
  • Central Asia
  • North Africa

It grows best in warm temperate and subtropical climates.

Cultivation

The tree prefers:

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Mild to warm climates

It is slow-growing but can live for many decades, producing fruit annually.

Uses

Black mulberries are used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and syrups
  • Dried fruit snacks
  • Wine and desserts

Nutritional Value

They contain:

  • Vitamin C
  • Iron
  • Antioxidants
  • Dietary fiber

Interesting Facts

  • Some trees are believed to be over 100 years old.
  • The fruit was highly valued in ancient Persia and Rome.
  • Leaves are also used to feed silkworms (related mulberry species).
