Guyabano (Soursop)

Guyabano, also known as soursop (Annona muricata), is a tropical fruit known for its creamy texture and sweet-tart flavor.

Description

The fruit is large, green, and covered in soft spines. Inside is white, fibrous pulp with a strong aromatic flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Caribbean
  • Central America
  • South America
  • Southeast Asia

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • High humidity
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun

Uses

  • Juices and smoothies
  • Desserts and ice cream
  • Traditional herbal uses

Interesting Facts

  • Flavor is often described as strawberry-pineapple-citrus.
  • The seeds are not edible.
