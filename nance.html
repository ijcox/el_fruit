Nance

Nance (Byrsonima crassifolia) is a small tropical fruit known for its strong aroma and sweet-tart flavor.

Description

The fruit is small, round, and yellow when ripe, with soft, oily pulp and one to several hard seeds inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Central America
  • South America
  • Caribbean
  • Tropical savannas

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • Dry to moderately wet soils
  • Full sun
  • Drought tolerant tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Fermented drinks
  • Jams and desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Very aromatic—often considered an acquired taste.
  • Popular in traditional Latin American cuisine.
