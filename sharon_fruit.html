Sharon Fruit

Sharon fruit is a seedless, non-astringent variety of persimmon known for its sweet honey-like flavor and smooth texture.

Description

The fruit is round or slightly flattened with thin orange skin and soft, jelly-like orange flesh when ripe.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Israel (commercial development)
  • Spain
  • South Korea
  • Warm temperate regions

Cultivation

  • Temperate to subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Deciduous fruit tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Desserts
  • Fruit salads

Interesting Facts

  • Named after the Sharon plain in Israel.
  • Unlike traditional persimmons, it can be eaten while still firm.
