Loganberry

Loganberry is a hybrid fruit created from a cross between blackberry and raspberry, known for its deep red color and tart flavor.

Description

The fruit is elongated and dark red to purple when ripe, with juicy flesh and a flavor that combines the sweetness of raspberries with the tartness of blackberries.

Distribution and Habitat

  • United States (origin)
  • United Kingdom
  • Europe (cultivated)

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun
  • Requires trellising support

Uses

  • Jams and preserves
  • Desserts
  • Juices and wines

Interesting Facts

  • Discovered accidentally in California in the late 1800s.
  • Named after horticulturist James H. Logan.
