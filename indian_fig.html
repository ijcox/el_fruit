Indian Fig

Indian fig, also known as prickly pear (Opuntia ficus-indica), is a cactus fruit widely grown in arid and semi-arid regions.

Description

The fruit is oval and covered in a thick skin with tiny spines (glochids). Inside is juicy, sweet flesh that can be red, orange, or yellow.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Mexico
  • Mediterranean region
  • North Africa
  • Southwestern United States

Cultivation

  • Dry, arid climates
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Full sun
  • Very drought tolerant

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Juices and syrups
  • Jams and candies

Interesting Facts

  • The plant is both edible and ornamental.
  • Care must be taken to remove spines before eating.
