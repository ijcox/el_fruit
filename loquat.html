Loquat

Loquat (Eriobotrya japonica) is a small, sweet fruit native to China, often enjoyed fresh or used in jams.

Description

The fruit is oval to round, with smooth yellow-orange skin and juicy flesh that can range from sweet to slightly tangy.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • Japan
  • Mediterranean region
  • United States (California)

Cultivation

  • Subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Mild winters

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and jellies
  • Desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Flowers in autumn and fruits in spring.
  • Seeds should not be eaten as they are toxic.
