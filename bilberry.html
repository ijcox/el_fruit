Bilberry

Bilberry (Vaccinium myrtillus) is a small, dark blue to nearly black berry closely related to the blueberry. It is native to Europe and parts of northern Asia, where it grows in wild forested areas.

Description

Bilberries are small, soft berries with a deep purple flesh inside and a slightly tart, sweet flavor. Unlike blueberries, the pigment runs through the entire fruit, staining fingers and mouths when eaten.

Distribution and Habitat

Bilberry grows in:

  • Northern and Central Europe
  • Scandinavia
  • Russia
  • Mountain regions of Asia

It prefers acidic soils and cool, temperate forest environments.

Cultivation

Bilberry plants grow best in:

  • Acidic, well-drained soils
  • Cool climates
  • Partial shade conditions

Most bilberries are wild-harvested rather than commercially farmed.

Uses

Bilberries are used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and preserves
  • Pies and desserts
  • Traditional herbal remedies

Nutritional Value

Bilberries are rich in:

  • Anthocyanins (strong antioxidants)
  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin K
  • Dietary fiber

Interesting Facts

  • Bilberries are often associated with improved night vision in folklore.
  • They are smaller and darker than blueberries.
  • The fruit is widely used in eye-health supplements.
