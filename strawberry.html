Strawberry

Strawberry (Fragaria × ananassa) is a widely loved fruit known for its bright red color, juicy texture, and sweet aroma.

Description

The strawberry is an aggregate fruit made up of many tiny seed-like achenes on its outer surface, with soft red flesh inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Europe (hybrid origin)
  • North America
  • South America
  • Temperate regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Cool to temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Low-growing perennial plant

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Desserts and cakes
  • Jams and smoothies

Interesting Facts

  • Strawberries are not true berries botanically.
  • Each “seed” on the outside is actually a separate fruit.
