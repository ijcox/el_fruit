Date Plum

Date plum (Diospyros lotus) is a species of persimmon known for its small, sweet fruits that resemble dates when dried. It has been cultivated since ancient times across parts of Asia and Europe.

Description

Date plums are small, round fruits that ripen from green to yellow or purple-black. The flesh becomes soft and sweet when fully ripe, often compared to dates or figs.

Distribution and Habitat

Date plum is native to:

  • Southwest Asia
  • Southeast Europe
  • China
  • Turkey

It grows well in temperate climates and can tolerate a range of soil types.

Cultivation

Date plum trees prefer:

  • Warm temperate climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moderate watering

They are hardy and can withstand cooler temperatures better than some other persimmon species.

Uses

Date plums are used for:

  • Fresh eating when ripe
  • Dried fruit (date-like texture)
  • Traditional sweets
  • Ornamental planting

Nutritional Value

They contain:

  • Natural sugars
  • Fiber
  • Vitamin C
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • Date plum is one of the ancestors of cultivated persimmons.
  • The fruit is astringent until fully ripe.
  • It was introduced to Europe by ancient civilizations.
