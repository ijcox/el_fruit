Indian Gooseberry

Indian gooseberry, also known as amla (Phyllanthus emblica), is a small green fruit widely used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Description

The fruit is round, light green, and very sour and bitter when raw. It contains a fibrous texture with a hard seed inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • India
  • Pakistan
  • Southeast Asia

Cultivation

  • Subtropical climates
  • Dry to moderate rainfall regions
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun

Uses

  • Pickles and chutneys
  • Juices and powders
  • Ayurvedic medicine

Interesting Facts

  • One of the richest natural sources of vitamin C.
  • Highly valued in Ayurveda for health benefits.
