Guava

Guava (Psidium guajava) is a tropical fruit known for its strong aroma, sweet flavor, and high nutritional value.

Description

Guava fruits are round or oval with green or yellow skin. The flesh can be white, pink, or red, containing many small edible seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

Guava is native to:

  • Central America
  • South America
  • The Caribbean

It is now widely grown in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide.

Cultivation

Guava trees prefer:

  • Warm tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate rainfall

They are hardy and drought-tolerant once established.

Uses

Guava is used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Juices and smoothies
  • Jams and jellies
  • Desserts and candies

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Very high Vitamin C
  • Fiber
  • Vitamin A
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • Guava contains more vitamin C than oranges.
  • Both the flesh and seeds are edible.
  • The leaves are used in traditional medicine and teas.
