Caqui (Persimmon)

Caqui is the Spanish and Portuguese name for persimmon, referring mainly to the fruit of Diospyros kaki. It is a sweet, soft fruit enjoyed fresh or dried, especially in East Asia and Mediterranean regions.

Description

Caqui fruits are round or slightly flattened, with smooth orange skin. The flesh ranges from firm and crisp to soft and jelly-like when fully ripe, depending on the variety.

Distribution and Habitat

Caqui is grown in:

  • Japan
  • China
  • Korea
  • Spain
  • Italy
  • Brazil

It thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers.

Cultivation

Persimmon trees prefer:

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained, fertile soil
  • Moderate rainfall

They are long-lived trees that can produce fruit for decades.

Uses

Caqui is used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Dried fruit snacks
  • Baking and desserts
  • Jams and preserves

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin C
  • Fiber
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • The fruit can be astringent if eaten unripe.
  • In Japan, dried persimmons are a traditional winter delicacy.
  • The tree is also valued as an ornamental plant.
