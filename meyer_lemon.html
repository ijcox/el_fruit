Meyer Lemon

Meyer lemon is a citrus hybrid believed to be a cross between a lemon and a mandarin orange, known for its sweeter, less acidic flavor.

Description

The fruit is rounder and smaller than a standard lemon, with thin deep-yellow to orange skin and fragrant, mildly sweet juice.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • United States
  • Mediterranean regions

Cultivation

  • Subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Container-friendly plant

Uses

  • Cooking and baking
  • Desserts
  • Beverages

Interesting Facts

  • Less acidic than standard lemons.
  • Popular in gourmet cooking and baking.
