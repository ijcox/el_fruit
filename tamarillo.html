Tamarillo

Tamarillo (Solanum betaceum), also known as tree tomato, is a small egg-shaped fruit with a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.

Description

The fruit has smooth red, orange, or yellow skin and soft juicy flesh with black edible seeds inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Andes region (South America)
  • Peru
  • Colombia
  • New Zealand (major producer)

Cultivation

  • Cool subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Fast-growing shrub or small tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating (with sugar)
  • Juices and sauces
  • Chutneys and desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Also called “tree tomato.”
  • Flavor can be sweet or tart depending on variety.
