Kaki (Persimmon)

Kaki is the Japanese name for persimmon (Diospyros kaki), a sweet orange fruit widely eaten across East Asia.

Description

The fruit is round or slightly flattened, with smooth orange skin and soft, sweet flesh that becomes jelly-like when fully ripe.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Japan
  • China
  • Korea
  • Mediterranean region

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate rainfall

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Dried fruit
  • Desserts and baking

Interesting Facts

  • Some varieties must be fully soft before eating.
  • Very popular in autumn harvest festivals in Asia.
