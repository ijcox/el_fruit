Prickly Pear

Prickly pear (Opuntia species) is a cactus fruit known for its vibrant color, sweet taste, and spiny outer skin.

Description

The fruit grows on cactus pads and is oval-shaped with a thick skin covered in tiny spines, containing juicy red, orange, or yellow flesh inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Mexico (origin)
  • Southwestern United States
  • Mediterranean region
  • Arid and semi-arid climates worldwide

Cultivation

  • Dry desert climates
  • Full sun
  • Very low water requirements
  • Hardy cactus plant

Uses

  • Fresh eating (peeled fruit)
  • Juices and syrups
  • Jams and candies

Interesting Facts

  • Both the fruit and cactus pads (nopales) are edible.
  • Spines must be carefully removed before eating.
