Entawak

Entawak (Artocarpus anisophyllus) is a rare tropical fruit native to Southeast Asia, closely related to jackfruit and breadfruit. It is prized for its rich, sweet pulp and edible seeds.

Description

The fruit is round to oval with a rough, spiky outer skin that turns yellow-brown when ripe. Inside, it contains orange to yellow flesh surrounding large seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

Entawak is native to:

  • Borneo
  • Malaysia
  • Indonesia
  • Brunei

It grows in tropical lowland rainforests.

Cultivation

Entawak trees prefer:

  • Hot, humid tropical climates
  • Deep, fertile soil
  • High rainfall
  • Partial shade when young

It is not widely cultivated commercially due to limited demand and slow growth.

Uses

Entawak is used for:

  • Fresh consumption
  • Roasted seeds (edible and nutritious)
  • Traditional food in rural areas

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Carbohydrates
  • Dietary fiber
  • Healthy fats (in seeds)
  • Vitamin C

Interesting Facts

  • It is closely related to jackfruit but much less widely known.
  • The seeds are often roasted and eaten like nuts.
  • The fruit is considered a delicacy in parts of Borneo.
