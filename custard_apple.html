Custard Apple

Custard apple refers to fruits from the genus Annona, especially Annona squamosa. It is known for its creamy, sweet flesh and segmented green exterior.

Description

The fruit is round to heart-shaped with green, knobbly skin. Inside is soft, white, custard-like flesh divided into segments containing black seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

Custard apple is grown in:

  • India
  • Brazil
  • Mexico
  • Thailand
  • Philippines
  • Australia

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Cultivation

Custard apple trees prefer:

  • Warm tropical climates
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Moderate rainfall
  • Full sun exposure

They are drought-tolerant once established.

Uses

Custard apple is used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Desserts and smoothies
  • Ice cream flavoring
  • Juices and shakes

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin B6
  • Fiber
  • Natural sugars

Interesting Facts

  • Sometimes called “sugar apple” in some regions.
  • The seeds are toxic if crushed or consumed.
  • It is highly valued for its rich, sweet flavor.
