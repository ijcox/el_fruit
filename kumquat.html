Kumquat

Kumquat (Citrus japonica) is a small citrus fruit that can be eaten whole, including its sweet peel and tart flesh.

Description

The fruit is small and oval or round, bright orange when ripe, with a thin edible skin and sour juicy interior.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China
  • Japan
  • Southeast Asia
  • Mediterranean region

Cultivation

  • Subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate watering

Uses

  • Fresh eating whole
  • Marmalades
  • Candied fruit
  • Garnishes

Interesting Facts

  • One of the few citrus fruits eaten whole.
  • Sweet rind balances its tart interior.
