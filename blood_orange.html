Blood Orange

Blood orange is a variety of orange (Citrus × sinensis) known for its deep red flesh, which is caused by the presence of anthocyanins—pigments rarely found in citrus fruits.

Description

Blood oranges resemble regular oranges on the outside but have striking crimson to burgundy-colored flesh inside. They have a sweet flavor with raspberry-like undertones.

Distribution and Habitat

Blood oranges are primarily grown in:

  • Italy (especially Sicily)
  • Spain
  • North Africa
  • California (USA)

They thrive in Mediterranean climates with warm days and cool nights.

Cultivation

Blood orange trees prefer:

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained, slightly acidic soil
  • Mild winters

Temperature variation between day and night enhances their red pigmentation.

Uses

Blood oranges are used in:

  • Fresh eating
  • Juices and smoothies
  • Desserts and sorbets
  • Salads and sauces

Nutritional Value

They are rich in:

  • Vitamin C
  • Antioxidants
  • Fiber
  • Anthocyanins

Interesting Facts

  • The red pigmentation develops best in cold nighttime temperatures.
  • There are several varieties including Moro, Tarocco, and Sanguinello.
  • They are often considered the most flavorful type of orange.
