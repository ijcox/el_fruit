Barbados Cherry

Barbados cherry (Malpighia emarginata), also known as acerola, is a small tropical fruit known for its extremely high vitamin C content. It is native to the Caribbean, Central America, and northern South America.

Description

The fruit is small, round to slightly lobed, and typically bright red when ripe. It resembles a miniature apple or cherry. The flesh is soft, juicy, and divided into three small segments, each containing a seed.

Distribution and Habitat

Barbados cherry is commonly found in:

  • The Caribbean
  • Brazil
  • Mexico
  • Florida (USA)
  • Tropical Asia (cultivated)

It thrives in warm tropical and subtropical climates.

Cultivation

The plant grows as a shrub or small tree and prefers:

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Warm temperatures

It is fast-growing and can produce fruit multiple times per year in ideal conditions.

Uses

Barbados cherry is widely used in:

  • Fresh eating (when fully ripe)
  • Juices and smoothies
  • Jams and jellies
  • Vitamin C supplements

Nutritional Value

This fruit is especially known for:

  • Extremely high vitamin C content
  • Vitamin A
  • Antioxidants
  • Dietary fiber

Interesting Facts

  • One of the richest natural sources of vitamin C.
  • The fruit must be eaten quickly after harvest due to short shelf life.
  • It is often used in nutritional supplements worldwide.
