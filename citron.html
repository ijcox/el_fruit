Citron

Citron (Citrus medica) is one of the original citrus species, known for its thick rind, strong fragrance, and minimal pulp. It has been cultivated for thousands of years, especially in Asia and the Mediterranean.

Description

Citron is a large, irregularly shaped citrus fruit with a thick, bumpy rind. Unlike most citrus fruits, it contains very little juice and mostly thick white pith and aromatic peel.

Distribution and Habitat

Citron is grown in:

  • India
  • China
  • Israel
  • Italy
  • Greece
  • Middle East regions

It thrives in warm, subtropical to Mediterranean climates.

Cultivation

Citron trees prefer:

  • Warm, frost-free climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun exposure
  • Moderate watering

They are slow-growing but long-lived trees.

Uses

Citron is used for:

  • Candied peel (succade)
  • Religious rituals (especially Jewish Sukkot)
  • Perfumes and essential oils
  • Flavoring and preserves

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Essential oils (limonene-rich)
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • One of the earliest citrus fruits cultivated by humans.
  • Used in religious ceremonies for thousands of years.
  • Some varieties are highly fragrant and used purely for aroma.
