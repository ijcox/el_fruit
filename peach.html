Peach

Peach (Prunus persica) is a sweet, juicy stone fruit known for its fuzzy skin and fragrant yellow or orange flesh.

Description

The fruit is round with soft fuzzy skin ranging from yellow to red tones and a large central pit surrounded by juicy flesh.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • United States
  • Europe
  • Temperate regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Requires winter chill

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Pies and desserts
  • Canning and preserves

Interesting Facts

  • One of the most widely cultivated stone fruits.
  • Closely related to nectarines.
