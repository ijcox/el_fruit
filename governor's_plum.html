Governor's Plum

Governor’s plum (Flacourtia indica) is a small tropical fruit-bearing tree known for its tart, edible fruits and thorny branches.

Description

The fruit is round, turning from green to red or dark purple when ripe. It contains soft, juicy pulp surrounding several small seeds, with a sweet-tart flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

Governor’s plum is native to:

  • Africa
  • South Asia
  • Southeast Asia

It is also naturalized in tropical regions worldwide.

Cultivation

The tree prefers:

  • Warm tropical climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun
  • Drought tolerance once established

Uses

Governor’s plum is used for:

  • Fresh eating (when ripe)
  • Jams and preserves
  • Traditional medicine

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Iron
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • The tree is often used as a natural hedge due to its thorns.
  • Unripe fruits are quite sour, becoming sweeter as they ripen.
  • It is hardy and grows in poor soils.
