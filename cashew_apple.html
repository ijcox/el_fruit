Cashew Apple

The cashew apple is the fleshy, pear-shaped accessory fruit that grows on the cashew tree (Anacardium occidentale). It is attached to the cashew nut, which grows externally at its base.

Description

The cashew apple is soft, juicy, and typically yellow, red, or orange when ripe. It has a sweet yet slightly astringent flavor and is highly perishable.

Distribution and Habitat

Cashew apple is found in:

  • Brazil (native region)
  • India
  • Vietnam
  • West Africa
  • Southeast Asia

It grows in tropical coastal and dry forest regions.

Cultivation

Cashew trees prefer:

  • Hot tropical climates
  • Sandy, well-drained soils
  • Low to moderate rainfall

The cashew apple develops alongside the cashew nut, which is actually the true fruit.

Uses

Cashew apple is used for:

  • Juices and beverages
  • Jams and syrups
  • Fermented drinks (alcoholic beverages)
  • Animal feed (in some regions)

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C (very high)
  • Antioxidants
  • Dietary fiber

Interesting Facts

  • The cashew nut grows outside the fruit, attached beneath it.
  • The fruit is highly perishable and rarely exported fresh.
  • It is often overlooked compared to the cashew nut.
