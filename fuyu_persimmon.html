Fuyu Persimmon

Fuyu persimmon is a non-astringent variety of persimmon (Diospyros kaki) known for its sweet flavor and ability to be eaten while still firm.

Description

Fuyu persimmons are squat, tomato-shaped fruits with smooth orange skin. Their flesh is crisp when firm and becomes softer and sweeter as it ripens.

Distribution and Habitat

Fuyu persimmons are widely grown in:

  • Japan
  • China
  • South Korea
  • United States (California)

They thrive in temperate climates with warm summers and mild winters.

Cultivation

Fuyu persimmon trees prefer:

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate watering
  • Cool winter dormancy

They are relatively hardy and easy to grow compared to astringent varieties.

Uses

Fuyu persimmons are used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Salads
  • Baking and desserts
  • Dried fruit snacks

Nutritional Value

They contain:

  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin C
  • Fiber
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • Unlike some persimmons, Fuyu can be eaten while still firm.
  • The fruit is often compared to a sweet, crunchy apple when unripe.
  • It is one of the most popular persimmon varieties worldwide.
