Casimiroa (White Sapote)

Casimiroa edulis, commonly known as white sapote, is a subtropical fruit native to Mexico and Central America. It is known for its soft, custard-like texture and sweet flavor.

Description

The fruit is round to oval with thin green skin that turns yellowish when ripe. The inside is creamy, soft, and pale yellow to white, resembling custard.

Distribution and Habitat

White sapote is grown in:

  • Mexico (native range)
  • Central America
  • California (USA)
  • Australia
  • New Zealand

It thrives in subtropical and mild temperate climates.

Cultivation

Casimiroa trees prefer:

  • Warm climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun to partial shade

They are relatively hardy once established but sensitive to frost when young.

Uses

White sapote is used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Desserts and smoothies
  • Ice cream flavoring
  • Traditional medicinal uses

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Potassium
  • Fiber
  • Small amounts of healthy fats

Interesting Facts

  • Sometimes called “Mexican apple.”
  • The fruit must be fully ripe to avoid bitterness.
  • Some varieties can cause mild drowsiness if eaten in large amounts.
