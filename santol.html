Santol

Santol (Sandoricum koetjape) is a tropical fruit valued for its thick rind and sweet to tart cottony pulp.

Description

The fruit is round and apple-sized with yellow to brown skin and white segmented pulp surrounding large seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Southeast Asia (origin)
  • Philippines
  • Thailand
  • Tropical lowlands

Cultivation

  • Warm humid climates
  • Full sun
  • Deep fertile soil
  • Large tropical tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Candies and preserves
  • Sour cooking dishes

Interesting Facts

  • Sometimes called cotton fruit because of the pulp texture.
  • Both sweet and sour varieties exist.
