Osage Orange

Osage orange (Maclura pomifera) is a distinctive fruit known for its rough green appearance and strong, inedible latex-like pulp.

Description

The fruit is large, round, and bumpy with a green, brain-like surface. It contains sticky white latex and many seeds inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Central United States (native)
  • Plains and river valleys
  • Naturalized in many regions

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Drought tolerant
  • Hardy tree species

Uses

  • Primarily ornamental
  • Historically used for hedgerows (“hedge apples”)
  • Wood used for fencing and tools

Interesting Facts

  • Not actually an orange or citrus fruit.
  • Named after the Osage Nation and its bright wood.
