Pulasan

Pulasan (Nephelium mutabile) is a tropical fruit closely related to rambutan, known for its sweet, juicy flesh and spiky red shell.

Description

The fruit is round with thick, soft spines on its red or dark purple skin, containing translucent, sweet flesh surrounding a seed.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Malaysia (origin)
  • Indonesia
  • Thailand
  • Tropical Southeast Asia

Cultivation

  • Humid tropical climates
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Rich, well-drained soil
  • Slow-growing fruit tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Fruit platters
  • Local desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Often considered sweeter than rambutan.
  • Harvested mainly in local Southeast Asian markets.
