Cape Gooseberry

Cape gooseberry (Physalis peruviana) is a small, bright orange fruit enclosed in a papery husk. It is also known as goldenberry and is native to South America.

Description

The fruit is round, smooth, and orange-yellow when ripe, typically wrapped in a lantern-like husk. It has a sweet-tart tropical flavor similar to pineapple and mango.

Distribution and Habitat

Cape gooseberry is grown in:

  • Peru and Colombia (native range)
  • South Africa
  • Kenya
  • India
  • New Zealand

It grows well in subtropical and high-altitude tropical regions.

Cultivation

The plant prefers:

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Mild to warm climates

It is a hardy, bushy plant that produces fruit for several months.

Uses

Cape gooseberries are used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and preserves
  • Desserts and cakes
  • Salads and garnishes

Nutritional Value

They contain:

  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin A
  • Antioxidants
  • Fiber

Interesting Facts

  • The husk naturally protects the fruit from pests.
  • It is sometimes called “Inca berry.”
  • Can be eaten fresh or dried like raisins.
