Serviceberry

Serviceberry (Amelanchier species) is a small North American fruit known for its sweet blueberry-like taste and early spring blossoms.

Description

The fruit is small, round, and dark purple to red when ripe, resembling blueberries but with a slightly almond-like flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

  • North America (native)
  • Europe
  • Cool temperate forests
  • Mountain and woodland areas

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Deciduous shrub or small tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Pies and jams
  • Wild food harvesting

Interesting Facts

  • Also called “Juneberry.”
  • One of the earliest fruits to ripen in North America.
