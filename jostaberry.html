Jostaberry

Jostaberry is a hybrid berry created by crossing blackcurrant, gooseberry, and other Ribes species.

Description

The fruit is dark purple to black when ripe, larger than a blackcurrant, with a tangy-sweet flavor and juicy pulp.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Europe (cultivated)
  • North America (cultivated)

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Cold tolerant shrub

Uses

  • Jams and jellies
  • Baking
  • Fresh eating

Interesting Facts

  • Developed in Germany in the 20th century.
  • Combines traits of gooseberries and currants.
