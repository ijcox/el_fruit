Mombin

Mombin refers to fruits from the genus Spondias, commonly known as hog plum, widely eaten in tropical regions.

Description

The fruit is small to medium-sized, oval, and turns yellow, orange, or red when ripe. It has a fibrous, juicy pulp with a tart-sweet flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Caribbean
  • Central America
  • South America
  • West Africa

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Dry to moderately wet soils
  • Fast-growing trees

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Juices
  • Jams and preserves

Interesting Facts

  • Different species include yellow mombin and red mombin.
  • Widely used in traditional drinks and street foods.
