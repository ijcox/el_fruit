Pear

Pear (Pyrus species) is a sweet, juicy fruit known for its soft texture and distinctive bell shape.

Description

The fruit is typically green, yellow, or red with smooth skin and soft, grainy flesh surrounding a central core.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Europe
  • Asia
  • North America
  • Temperate regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Cold winter required for fruiting

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Cooking and baking
  • Juices and preserves

Interesting Facts

  • Some pears are eaten when firm, others when soft-ripe.
  • Ancient fruit cultivated for thousands of years.
