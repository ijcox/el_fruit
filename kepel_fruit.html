Kepel Fruit

Kepel fruit (Stelechocarpus burahol) is a rare tropical fruit native to Southeast Asia, traditionally associated with royal gardens in Java.

Description

The fruit is oval-shaped with a brownish outer skin and soft, fragrant orange pulp inside. It has a sweet, floral aroma.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Indonesia (Java)
  • Southeast Asia tropical forests

Cultivation

  • Tropical rainforest climate
  • High humidity
  • Deep, fertile soil
  • Partial shade

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Traditional aromatic use
  • Cultural and ceremonial planting

Interesting Facts

  • Historically planted in royal palace gardens in Java.
  • Believed to have deodorizing properties in traditional culture.
