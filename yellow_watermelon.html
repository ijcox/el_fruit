Yellow Watermelon

Yellow watermelon is a naturally occurring or selectively bred variety of watermelon with bright yellow flesh instead of the traditional red.

Description

The fruit has a thick green rind similar to regular watermelon, but the interior flesh is yellow, sweet, and often described as honey-like in flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Africa (origin of watermelons)
  • North America
  • Asia
  • Warm tropical and subtropical regions

Cultivation

  • Hot sunny climates
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Full sun exposure
  • Vining annual plant

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Fruit salads
  • Juices and smoothies

Interesting Facts

  • Yellow flesh is actually the original wild watermelon color.
  • Tends to be slightly sweeter but less acidic than red varieties.
