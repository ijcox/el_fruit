Red Currant

Red currant (Ribes rubrum) is a small translucent berry known for its bright tart flavor and vivid red clusters.

Description

The fruit grows in hanging strings of glossy red berries with juicy flesh and tiny edible seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Europe
  • Northern Asia
  • North America (cultivated)
  • Cool temperate climates

Cultivation

  • Cool climates
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moist fertile soil
  • Deciduous shrub

Uses

  • Jellies and jams
  • Desserts
  • Sauces and garnishes

Interesting Facts

  • Often too tart for many people to eat plain.
  • Popular in European baking traditions.
