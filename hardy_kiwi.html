Hardy Kiwi

Hardy kiwi (Actinidia arguta) is a small, smooth-skinned relative of the kiwi fruit that can be eaten whole.

Description

It is grape-sized, green, and sweet with edible skin and soft pulp similar to kiwi but less acidic.

Distribution and Habitat

  • East Asia
  • Russia
  • North America (cultivated)

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Fruit salads
  • Jams

Interesting Facts

  • Much more cold-hardy than standard kiwi.
  • Can survive sub-zero winters.
