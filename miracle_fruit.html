Miracle Fruit

Miracle fruit (Synsepalum dulcificum) is a unique berry known for its ability to temporarily alter taste perception, making sour foods taste sweet.

Description

The fruit is small, red, and berry-like with a single seed. Its pulp contains miraculin, a protein that modifies taste receptors.

Distribution and Habitat

  • West Africa (origin)
  • Tropical regions
  • Greenhouse cultivation worldwide

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • Acidic soil
  • High humidity
  • Shade-tolerant shrub

Uses

  • Flavor-tripping experiences
  • Dietary novelty fruit
  • Research in taste modification

Interesting Facts

  • Does not change food itself—only perception of taste.
  • Effects can last up to 1–2 hours.
