Orange

Orange (Citrus sinensis) is one of the world’s most popular citrus fruits, known for its juicy, sweet-tart flavor and high vitamin C content.

Description

The fruit is round with a bright orange peel and segmented juicy flesh inside, typically sweet with a refreshing citrus aroma.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • Brazil
  • United States (Florida, California)
  • Mediterranean region

Cultivation

  • Subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Frost-sensitive trees

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Juice production
  • Desserts and cooking

Interesting Facts

  • Most commercially grown citrus fruit in the world.
  • Hybrids of mandarin and pomelo ancestors.
