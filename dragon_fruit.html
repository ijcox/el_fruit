Dragon Fruit

Dragon fruit, also known as pitaya, is the fruit of several cactus species in the genus Hylocereus and Selenicereus. It is known for its vibrant appearance and mildly sweet flavor.

Description

Dragon fruit has bright pink or yellow skin with green, scale-like protrusions. The flesh inside is typically white or red with tiny black seeds, similar in texture to kiwi.

Distribution and Habitat

Originally native to Central and South America, dragon fruit is now widely grown in:

  • Vietnam
  • Thailand
  • Mexico
  • Israel
  • United States (California, Florida)

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Cultivation

Dragon fruit plants prefer:

  • Warm climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Low to moderate watering

As climbing cacti, they often require support structures.

Uses

Dragon fruit is used for:

  • Fresh consumption
  • Smoothies and juices
  • Desserts
  • Salads and decorative plating

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Fiber
  • Antioxidants
  • Magnesium

Interesting Facts

  • The flowers bloom at night and are pollinated by bats and moths.
  • It is sometimes called “strawberry pear.”
  • The seeds are edible and add a crunchy texture.
