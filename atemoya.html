Atemoya

Atemoya (Annona × atemoya) is a hybrid fruit resulting from a cross between the sugar apple (Annona squamosa) and the cherimoya (Annona cherimola). It is prized for its creamy texture and sweet, tropical flavor.

Description

Atemoya fruits are heart-shaped or rounded with a green, bumpy skin. The flesh inside is white, soft, and custard-like, containing several black seeds. The flavor is often described as a blend of pineapple, banana, and vanilla.

Distribution and Habitat

Atemoya is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions, including:

  • Florida (USA)
  • Brazil
  • Israel
  • Australia
  • Southeast Asia

It thrives in warm climates with moderate humidity.

Cultivation

Atemoya trees are small to medium-sized and prefer:

  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun exposure
  • Warm temperatures

They are often hand-pollinated in commercial orchards to improve fruit yield and quality.

Uses

Atemoya is typically eaten fresh due to its delicate texture:

  • Fresh consumption (scooped from the fruit)
  • Smoothies and desserts
  • Ice creams and custards

Nutritional Value

Atemoya contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Dietary fiber
  • Potassium

It is a nutritious and energy-rich fruit with natural sugars.

Interesting Facts

  • The name "atemoya" combines parts of its parent fruits' names.
  • It is considered one of the best-tasting fruits in the Annona family.
  • The seeds are not edible and should be removed before consumption.
