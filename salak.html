Salak

Salak, also called snake fruit, is a tropical fruit known for its reddish-brown scaly skin and crisp sweet-tart flesh.

Description

The fruit is small and bulb-shaped with thin skin resembling snake scales, containing segmented pale flesh with a crunchy texture.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Indonesia (origin)
  • Java
  • Sumatra
  • Tropical Southeast Asia

Cultivation

  • Hot humid climates
  • Partial sun
  • Moist fertile soil
  • Grows on thorny palm plants

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Candied fruit
  • Juices and preserves

Interesting Facts

  • Called snake fruit because of its skin texture.
  • Flavor can resemble apple, pineapple, or banana depending on variety.
