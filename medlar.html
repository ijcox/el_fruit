Medlar

Medlar (Mespilus germanica) is an old-world fruit known for its unique ripening process called “bletting,” where it becomes soft and sweet after harvest.

Description

The fruit is small, brown, and apple-like, with firm flesh that only becomes edible when overripe and softened.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Europe
  • Western Asia
  • Mediterranean regions

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Cold hardy tree

Uses

  • Jams and jellies
  • Wine and preserves
  • Traditional desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Must be softened before eating (bletted).
  • Popular in medieval European cuisine.
