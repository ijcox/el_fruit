Rambutan

Rambutan (Nephelium lappaceum) is a tropical fruit famous for its hairy red shell and sweet translucent flesh.

Description

The fruit is round or oval with a bright red or yellow skin covered in soft flexible spines, enclosing juicy white flesh around a seed.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Malaysia (origin)
  • Indonesia
  • Thailand
  • Tropical Southeast Asia

Cultivation

  • Hot humid tropical climates
  • Rich well-drained soil
  • Regular rainfall
  • Evergreen fruit tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Fruit salads
  • Desserts and preserves

Interesting Facts

  • The name means “hairy” in Malay.
  • Closely related to lychee and pulasan.
