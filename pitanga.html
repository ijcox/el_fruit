Pitanga

Pitanga (Eugenia uniflora), also known as Surinam cherry, is a small tropical fruit with a strong aroma and sweet-tart flavor.

Description

The fruit is ribbed and pumpkin-shaped, turning from green to bright red or deep purple-black when ripe, with juicy soft flesh inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • South America (origin)
  • Brazil
  • Caribbean
  • Tropical regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Tropical and subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Hardy shrub or small tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and jellies
  • Juices and desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Leaves are aromatic when crushed.
  • Fruit flavor varies widely—can be sweet or resinous.
