Jaboticaba

Jaboticaba is a unique fruit that grows directly on the trunk of its tree (Plinia cauliflora), native to Brazil.

Description

The fruit is small, round, and dark purple to black when ripe. Its white, grape-like pulp is sweet and jelly-like.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Brazil
  • Paraguay
  • Bolivia

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • Moist, acidic soil
  • High humidity
  • Partial to full sun

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and jellies
  • Wines and liqueurs

Interesting Facts

  • Fruits grow directly on the trunk (cauliflory).
  • Very perishable, often eaten soon after harvest.
