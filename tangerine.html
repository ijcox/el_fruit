Tangerine

Tangerine (Citrus reticulata) is a sweet, easy-to-peel citrus fruit known for its bright flavor and fragrant aroma.

Description

The fruit is small to medium-sized with deep orange skin that peels easily and juicy segmented flesh inside, often sweeter than oranges.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • Japan
  • Mediterranean region
  • Subtropical citrus-growing areas

Cultivation

  • Warm subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Citrus tree species

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Juices
  • Desserts and salads

Interesting Facts

  • Named after Tangier, Morocco (historical export port).
  • One of the most widely consumed citrus fruits globally.
