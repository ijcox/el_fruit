Strawberry Guava

Strawberry guava (Psidium cattleianum) is a small tropical fruit known for its sweet-tart flavor and strawberry-like aroma.

Description

The fruit is round or oval with smooth red or yellow skin and juicy pink or white pulp containing many small seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Brazil (origin)
  • Hawaii
  • Caribbean
  • Tropical and subtropical regions

Cultivation

  • Warm climates
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Moist well-drained soil
  • Evergreen shrub or small tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and jellies
  • Juices and desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Highly invasive in some tropical regions.
  • Leaves are also used for medicinal teas.
