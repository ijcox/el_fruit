Pomelo

Pomelo (Citrus maxima) is the largest citrus fruit, known for its thick rind, mild sweetness, and less acidic flavor compared to grapefruit.

Description

The fruit is large and round with a thick green or yellow rind and pale yellow to pink segmented flesh inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Southeast Asia (origin)
  • China
  • Thailand
  • Tropical and subtropical regions

Cultivation

  • Warm tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Slow-growing citrus tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Salads and desserts
  • Citrus flavoring

Interesting Facts

  • Ancestor of the modern grapefruit.
  • Can grow much larger than grapefruits.
