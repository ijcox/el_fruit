Quince

Quince (Cydonia oblonga) is a fragrant yellow fruit known for its hard texture when raw and rich aroma when cooked.

Description

The fruit is similar in shape to a pear or apple, with golden-yellow skin and very firm, tart flesh that becomes soft and sweet when cooked.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Caucasus region (origin)
  • Iran
  • Turkey
  • Mediterranean region

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Deciduous fruit tree

Uses

  • Jams and jellies
  • Roasted or stewed dishes
  • Traditional desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Raw quince is usually too hard and sour to eat.
  • One of the oldest cultivated fruits in history.
