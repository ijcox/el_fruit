Persian Lime

Persian lime (Citrus × latifolia) is a seedless, juicy citrus fruit widely used in cooking and beverages around the world.

Description

The fruit is medium-sized, round to oval, with smooth green skin that turns yellow when fully ripe, and acidic, juicy flesh inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Persia (historical origin)
  • Mexico
  • United States (Florida, California)
  • Tropical and subtropical regions

Cultivation

  • Warm climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Frost-sensitive citrus tree

Uses

  • Cooking and seasoning
  • Beverages and cocktails
  • Desserts and marinades

Interesting Facts

  • Most commonly sold lime in grocery stores.
  • Usually seedless compared to other lime types.
