Hackberry

Hackberry refers to several species of trees in the genus Celtis that produce small, edible berries.

Description

Hackberry fruits are small, round drupes that turn from green to orange or dark purple when ripe. They have a sweet, date-like flavor with a large seed inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • North America
  • Europe
  • Asia

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Adaptable to many soil types
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Drought tolerant once established

Uses

  • Wild foraging
  • Wildlife food source
  • Traditional food use

Interesting Facts

  • Very important food source for birds.
  • Can survive in harsh urban environments.
