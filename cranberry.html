Cranberry

Cranberry is a small, tart red berry from low-growing shrubs in the genus Vaccinium. It is widely known for its use in sauces, juices, and health-related products.

Description

Cranberries are small, round, glossy red fruits with a very sour taste when raw. They grow on creeping vines in wetland areas called bogs.

Distribution and Habitat

Cranberries are native to and cultivated in:

  • United States (Wisconsin, Massachusetts, New Jersey)
  • Canada
  • Northern Europe

They thrive in acidic peat bogs and wetlands in cool climates.

Cultivation

Cranberry plants prefer:

  • Cold climates
  • Acidic, sandy peat soil
  • High water availability
  • Low nutrient environments

Fields are often flooded during harvest to float berries for easier collection.

Uses

Cranberries are used for:

  • Cranberry sauce
  • Juices and cocktails
  • Dried snacks
  • Jams and jellies
  • Health supplements

Nutritional Value

They contain:

  • Vitamin C
  • Antioxidants (proanthocyanidins)
  • Fiber
  • Low natural sugar

Interesting Facts

  • Cranberries float due to air pockets inside the fruit.
  • They are a traditional Thanksgiving food in the United States.
  • Used historically by Indigenous peoples for food and medicine.
