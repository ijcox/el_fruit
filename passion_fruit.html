Passion Fruit

Passion fruit (Passiflora edulis) is a tropical fruit known for its aromatic pulp, tart-sweet flavor, and edible seeds.

Description

The fruit is round or oval with a tough purple or yellow rind and a juicy interior filled with golden pulp and black seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

  • South America (origin)
  • Brazil
  • Australia
  • Africa and Asia (cultivated)

Cultivation

  • Tropical and subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Climbing vine plant

Uses

  • Juices and smoothies
  • Desserts
  • Flavoring for drinks and sauces

Interesting Facts

  • The name comes from Christian symbolism, not taste.
  • Both pulp and seeds are edible.
