Bitter Melon

Bitter melon (Momordica charantia), also known as bitter gourd or karela, is a tropical vine fruit widely used as a vegetable in Asian, African, and Caribbean cuisine. It is known for its distinct bitter taste and medicinal properties.

Description

Bitter melon is an oblong, warty fruit that resembles a cucumber or gourd. Its color ranges from light green to yellow-orange when fully ripe. Inside, it contains soft red seeds embedded in a spongy white pith.

Distribution and Habitat

Bitter melon is grown in:

  • India
  • China
  • Southeast Asia
  • Caribbean islands
  • Africa

It thrives in warm, humid tropical climates.

Cultivation

The plant prefers:

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Warm, humid conditions

It is a fast-growing climbing vine that requires trellises for support.

Uses

Bitter melon is commonly used in:

  • Stir-fries and curries
  • Soups and stews
  • Juices and herbal drinks
  • Traditional medicine

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin A
  • Fiber
  • Plant compounds with blood sugar-regulating properties

Interesting Facts

  • It is one of the most bitter edible fruits in the world.
  • Used in traditional medicine for diabetes management.
  • The bitterness decreases slightly when cooked.
