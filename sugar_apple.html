Sugar Apple

Sugar apple (Annona squamosa) is a tropical fruit known for its sweet custard-like flesh and knobby green skin.

Description

The fruit is round to heart-shaped with segmented green skin that separates easily when ripe, revealing soft creamy white pulp with black seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Central America (origin)
  • South America
  • India
  • Tropical regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Hot tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Deciduous tropical tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Desserts and ice cream
  • Smoothies and beverages

Interesting Facts

  • Also called “sweetsop.”
  • Closely related to soursop and cherimoya.
