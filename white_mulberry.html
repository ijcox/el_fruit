White Mulberry

White mulberry (Morus alba) is a fast-growing tree known for its sweet berries and its historical role in silkworm cultivation.

Description

The fruit is long and cylindrical, white to pale pink when ripe, becoming sweet and soft with a mild honey-like flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • Asia
  • Europe
  • North America (introduced)

Cultivation

  • Temperate to subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Adaptable to many soil types
  • Fast-growing deciduous tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and wines
  • Silkworm feed (leaves)

Interesting Facts

  • Key plant in silk production history.
  • Can become invasive in some regions.
