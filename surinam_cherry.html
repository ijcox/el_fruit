Surinam Cherry

Surinam cherry (Eugenia uniflora) is a tropical fruit known for its ribbed red-to-dark purple fruit and sweet-tart flavor.

Description

The fruit is small and pumpkin-shaped with deep ridges, containing juicy flesh that can range from sweet to resinous depending on ripeness.

Distribution and Habitat

  • South America (Brazil, Uruguay)
  • Caribbean
  • Tropical and subtropical regions
  • Ornamental hedges worldwide

Cultivation

  • Warm climates
  • Full sun to partial shade
  • Well-drained soil
  • Evergreen shrub or small tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating (when fully ripe)
  • Jams and jellies
  • Ornamental landscaping

Interesting Facts

  • Flavor can vary dramatically between fruits on the same plant.
  • Leaves are aromatic when crushed.
