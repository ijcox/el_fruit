Mayhaw

Mayhaw is a small fruit from hawthorn trees (Crataegus species), native to the southern United States and known for its use in jellies.

Description

The fruit is small, round, and usually red or yellow when ripe, with a tart flavor and several small seeds inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Southern United States
  • Wetlands and swamps
  • Riverside areas

Cultivation

  • Temperate to subtropical climates
  • Moist soil conditions
  • Full sun
  • Flood-tolerant tree species

Uses

  • Jellies and preserves
  • Syrups
  • Traditional recipes

Interesting Facts

  • Traditionally harvested in May, giving it its name.
  • Closely related to apples and pears in the rose family.
