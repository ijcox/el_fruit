Tropical Almond

Tropical almond (Terminalia catappa) is a large tropical tree that produces edible seeds and almond-like kernels inside its fruit.

Description

The fruit is flat and oval-shaped with a fibrous outer layer that surrounds a hard shell containing an edible seed kernel.

Distribution and Habitat

  • South Asia (origin)
  • Southeast Asia
  • Pacific Islands
  • Tropical coastal regions

Cultivation

  • Hot coastal climates
  • Full sun
  • Sandy or well-drained soil
  • Large spreading shade tree

Uses

  • Edible seeds (raw or roasted)
  • Traditional medicine
  • Ornamental shade tree

Interesting Facts

  • Leaves turn bright red before falling.
  • Often planted as a coastal windbreak tree.
