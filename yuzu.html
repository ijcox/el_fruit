Yuzu

Yuzu is a highly aromatic East Asian citrus fruit known for its strong fragrance, tart juice, and culinary versatility in Japanese, Korean, and Chinese cuisine.

Description

The fruit is small and round with a thick yellow to green bumpy rind and highly acidic, seedy pulp inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • Japan
  • Korea
  • East Asian temperate regions

Cultivation

  • Cool to mild temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Hardy citrus shrub or small tree

Uses

  • Juices and sauces
  • Yuzu kosho (spicy paste)
  • Flavoring for desserts and drinks

Interesting Facts

  • Extremely fragrant rind used for zest.
  • Often used during winter solstice baths in Japan.
