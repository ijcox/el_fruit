Lucuma

Lucuma (Pouteria lucuma) is a subtropical fruit native to South America, prized for its sweet, maple-like flavor.

Description

The fruit is round to oval with green skin and dry, yellow-orange flesh that has a creamy texture and unique flavor often compared to sweet potato or caramel.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Peru
  • Chile
  • Ecuador
  • Andean regions

Cultivation

  • Subtropical climates
  • High-altitude regions
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun

Uses

  • Ice cream flavoring
  • Desserts
  • Powdered sweetener

Interesting Facts

  • Known as the “Gold of the Incas.”
  • Very popular flavor in Peruvian desserts.
