Rose Hip

Rose hip is the bright fruit of rose plants, valued for its tart flavor and exceptionally high vitamin C content.

Description

The fruit is small, oval or round, usually red to orange, forming after rose flowers fade and containing many tiny seeds inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Europe
  • Asia
  • North America
  • Temperate climates worldwide

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Produced on many wild and cultivated roses

Uses

  • Teas and syrups
  • Jams and marmalades
  • Supplements and powders

Interesting Facts

  • Often harvested after frost for sweeter flavor.
  • Used historically as a vitamin source during wartime shortages.
