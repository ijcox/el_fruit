Fig

Figs are soft, sweet fruits produced by trees in the genus Ficus. They are one of the oldest cultivated fruits in human history.

Description

Figs are small pear-shaped fruits with soft skin that ranges from green to purple or brown when ripe. Inside, they contain a sweet, jam-like flesh filled with tiny edible seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

Figs are native to:

  • Western Asia
  • Middle East
  • Mediterranean region

They are now widely grown in:

  • Europe
  • North America
  • Australia
  • South America

Cultivation

Fig trees prefer:

  • Warm, dry summers
  • Mild winters
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun

They are drought-tolerant once established.

Uses

Figs are used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Dried fruit snacks
  • Baking and desserts
  • Jams and preserves

Nutritional Value

They contain:

  • Fiber
  • Potassium
  • Calcium
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • Figs are technically inverted flowers, not true fruits.
  • They are pollinated by tiny fig wasps.
  • They have been cultivated for over 5,000 years.
