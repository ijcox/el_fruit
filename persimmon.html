Persimmon

Persimmon (Diospyros species) is a sweet, orange-colored fruit known for its honey-like flavor when fully ripe.

Description

The fruit is round or oval with smooth orange skin and soft, jelly-like flesh when ripe, or astringent and firm when unripe.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • Japan
  • Korea
  • United States (cultivated)

Cultivation

  • Temperate to subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Hardy deciduous trees

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Desserts and baking
  • Dried fruit snacks

Interesting Facts

  • Some varieties are only edible when fully soft-ripe.
  • The name means “divine fruit” in Greek origin of the genus.
