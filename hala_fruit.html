Hala Fruit

Hala fruit comes from the pandanus tree (Pandanus tectorius) and is native to tropical Pacific islands. It is known for its unique segmented structure.

Description

The fruit is made up of multiple wedge-shaped segments called keys. It is orange to red when ripe and has a fibrous, sweet aroma.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Hawaii
  • Polynesia
  • Micronesia
  • Southeast Asia

Cultivation

  • Tropical coastal regions
  • Sandy soils
  • High salt tolerance
  • Full sun

Uses

  • Chewing raw segments
  • Traditional food
  • Flavoring and natural dyes

Interesting Facts

  • The tree is also used for weaving mats and baskets.
  • The fruit segments are often chewed rather than fully eaten.
