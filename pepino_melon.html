Pepino Melon

Pepino melon (Solanum muricatum) is a sweet, aromatic fruit native to South America with a flavor resembling melon and pear.

Description

The fruit is oval-shaped with smooth yellow skin often marked with purple streaks, and soft juicy flesh inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Andes region (origin)
  • South America
  • New Zealand
  • Warm temperate regions

Cultivation

  • Mild temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Frost-sensitive plant

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Fruit salads
  • Desserts and smoothies

Interesting Facts

  • Related to tomatoes and potatoes (nightshade family).
  • Tastes sweeter when fully ripe.
