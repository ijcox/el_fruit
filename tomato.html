Tomato

Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum) is a widely cultivated fruit commonly used as a vegetable in cooking, known for its juicy flesh and versatile flavor.

Description

The fruit is round or oval, usually red when ripe, with soft juicy flesh and many small seeds inside gelatinous chambers.

Distribution and Habitat

  • South America (origin)
  • Mexico
  • Worldwide cultivation
  • Warm temperate and tropical regions

Cultivation

  • Warm climates
  • Full sun
  • Rich, well-drained soil
  • Annual plant in most climates

Uses

  • Sauces and soups
  • Salads and fresh eating
  • Processed foods (ketchup, paste)

Interesting Facts

  • Botanically a fruit, but legally considered a vegetable in some countries.
  • Part of the nightshade family.
