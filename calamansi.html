Calamansi

Calamansi (Citrus × microcarpa) is a small citrus fruit widely used in Southeast Asian cuisine. It is known for its tangy flavor and aromatic juice, often used as a seasoning or beverage base.

Description

Calamansi is a small, round citrus fruit resembling a lime or small mandarin. Its skin is thin and turns from green to orange when fully ripe. The juice is highly acidic and fragrant.

Distribution and Habitat

Calamansi is primarily found in:

  • Philippines
  • Malaysia
  • Indonesia
  • Southern China

It grows best in tropical and subtropical climates.

Cultivation

Calamansi trees prefer:

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Warm, humid conditions

They are commonly grown in home gardens and small orchards.

Uses

Calamansi is used for:

  • Juices and drinks
  • Marinades and sauces
  • Seasoning for seafood and meats
  • Traditional remedies

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C (high concentration)
  • Antioxidants
  • Citric acid

Interesting Facts

  • Often called “Philippine lime.”
  • Juice is commonly used as a daily table condiment in the Philippines.
  • The fruit is both ornamental and edible.
