Elephant Apple

Elephant apple (Dillenia indica) is a large, sour fruit native to South and Southeast Asia. It is named for its large size, which is sometimes comparable to the weight an elephant might carry or consume in folklore.

Description

The fruit is round, green, and can grow quite large. It has a thick, fibrous outer skin and soft, acidic pulp inside containing multiple seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

Elephant apple is native to:

  • India
  • Bangladesh
  • Sri Lanka
  • Myanmar
  • Thailand

It grows in tropical forests and riverbanks.

Cultivation

Elephant apple trees prefer:

  • Tropical climates
  • High rainfall
  • Moist, fertile soil
  • Full sun to partial shade

They are often grown as ornamental and fruit-bearing trees.

Uses

Elephant apple is used for:

  • Culinary dishes (curries and chutneys)
  • Pickles and preserves
  • Traditional medicine
  • Juices and flavoring

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Fiber
  • Antioxidants
  • Organic acids

Interesting Facts

  • The fruit is too sour to eat raw in most cases.
  • It is widely used in traditional Indian cuisine.
  • The large leaves of the tree are also notable and ornamental.
