White Currant

White currant is a small translucent berry, a variety of red currant (Ribes rubrum) lacking the red pigment, known for its sweet and mild flavor.

Description

The berries are small, round, and pearl-white to pale yellow, growing in clusters on deciduous shrubs.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Europe (origin)
  • United Kingdom
  • North America (cultivated)
  • Temperate regions

Cultivation

  • Cool temperate climates
  • Partial sun
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Deciduous berry shrub

Uses

  • Jellies and jams
  • Desserts and baking
  • Fresh snacking

Interesting Facts

  • Less acidic than red currants.
  • Often used in gourmet desserts due to its delicate flavor.
