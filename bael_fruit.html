Bael Fruit

Bael fruit (Aegle marmelos) is a sacred and medicinal fruit native to the Indian subcontinent. It is widely used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine and has cultural significance in Hindu traditions.

Description

Bael fruit has a hard, woody shell that turns yellowish when ripe. Inside is a thick, aromatic pulp that is sticky and orange-brown in color, containing numerous seeds embedded in a gelatinous matrix.

Distribution and Habitat

Bael is found in:

  • India
  • Bangladesh
  • Nepal
  • Sri Lanka
  • Southeast Asia

It grows well in dry tropical and subtropical climates.

Cultivation

Bael trees are hardy and prefer:

  • Hot, dry climates
  • Sandy or loamy soil
  • Full sun exposure

The tree is drought-resistant and can survive in poor soil conditions.

Uses

Bael fruit is used in:

  • Traditional Ayurvedic medicine
  • Juices and sherbets
  • Digestive remedies
  • Religious offerings

Nutritional Value

Bael fruit contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Fiber
  • Tannins
  • Natural antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • Considered a sacred tree in Hinduism.
  • Its leaves are often used in religious rituals.
  • The fruit is valued for digestive health benefits.
