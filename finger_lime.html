Finger Lime

Finger lime (Citrus australasica) is a unique citrus fruit native to Australia, known for its elongated shape and caviar-like juice vesicles.

Description

Finger limes are small, cylindrical fruits that can be green, purple, or reddish. Inside, they contain tiny juice pearls that burst with a tangy citrus flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

Finger lime is native to:

  • Eastern Australia

It grows in subtropical and rainforest environments.

Cultivation

Finger lime trees prefer:

  • Warm subtropical climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Partial shade to full sun
  • Moderate watering

They are slow-growing but increasingly cultivated for gourmet markets.

Uses

Finger lime is used for:

  • Gourmet cuisine garnish
  • Seafood dishes
  • Drinks and cocktails
  • Desserts

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Antioxidants
  • Fiber
  • Citrus acids

Interesting Facts

  • It is often called “citrus caviar.”
  • The fruit is highly prized by chefs worldwide.
  • It was traditionally used by Indigenous Australians.
