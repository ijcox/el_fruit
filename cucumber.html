Cucumber

Cucumber (Cucumis sativus) is a widely cultivated plant in the gourd family. Although commonly used as a vegetable in culinary contexts, it is botanically a fruit because it develops from a flower and contains seeds.

Description

Cucumbers are elongated, cylindrical fruits with green skin and watery, crisp flesh. They are typically eaten raw or pickled.

Distribution and Habitat

Cucumbers are grown worldwide, especially in:

  • China (largest producer)
  • India
  • United States
  • Turkey
  • Russia

They grow best in warm, temperate to tropical climates.

Cultivation

Cucumber plants prefer:

  • Warm temperatures
  • Full sun
  • Rich, well-drained soil
  • Regular watering

They grow on vines or bush varieties depending on cultivation type.

Uses

Cucumbers are used for:

  • Fresh salads
  • Pickles
  • Juices and smoothies
  • Skincare and cosmetics

Nutritional Value

They contain:

  • High water content (~95%)
  • Vitamin K
  • Small amounts of vitamin C
  • Electrolytes

Interesting Facts

  • Cucumbers are cooling due to high water content.
  • They are botanically classified as berries.
  • Ancient civilizations in India cultivated cucumbers over 3,000 years ago.
