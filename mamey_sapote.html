Mamey Sapote

Mamey sapote (Pouteria sapota) is a tropical fruit known for its sweet, creamy texture and rich flavor often compared to pumpkin and sweet potato.

Description

The fruit is large and oval with rough brown skin and soft orange to reddish flesh. It contains a large, shiny brown seed.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Central America
  • Mexico
  • Caribbean
  • South Florida

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Warm temperatures

Uses

  • Smoothies and milkshakes
  • Desserts
  • Fresh eating

Interesting Facts

  • Highly valued in Latin American cuisine.
  • Often used in ice creams and beverages.
