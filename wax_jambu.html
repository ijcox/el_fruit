Wax Jambu

Wax jambu (Syzygium samarangense) is another name for wax apple, a crisp and watery tropical fruit known for its refreshing taste.

Description

The fruit is bell-shaped with glossy wax-like skin ranging from pale green to deep red, and a hollow crunchy interior with mild sweetness.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Southeast Asia (origin)
  • Malaysia
  • Indonesia
  • Tropical regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Warm humid climates
  • Full sun
  • Fertile well-drained soil
  • Evergreen tropical tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Salads and fruit platters
  • Juices

Interesting Facts

  • “Wax jambu” and “wax apple” refer to the same species.
  • Very popular street fruit in Southeast Asia.
