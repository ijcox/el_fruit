Tangelo

Tangelo is a citrus hybrid fruit created by crossing a tangerine with a pomelo or grapefruit, known for its juiciness and sweet-tart flavor.

Description

The fruit is bright orange, slightly bell-shaped, with loose skin that peels easily and very juicy segmented flesh inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • United States (Florida, California)
  • Israel
  • South Africa
  • Subtropical citrus-growing regions

Cultivation

  • Warm subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Citrus hybrid tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Juices
  • Fruit salads

Interesting Facts

  • Name comes from “tangerine” + “pomelo.”
  • Often juicier than standard oranges.
