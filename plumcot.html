Plumcot

Plumcot is a hybrid fruit created from crossing a plum and an apricot, combining the sweetness of apricots with the richness of plums.

Description

The fruit varies in appearance, often round with smooth skin that can be red, purple, or orange, and juicy flesh with a balanced sweet-tart flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

  • United States (hybrid development)
  • Europe
  • Temperate orchards worldwide

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Requires careful grafting or hybrid cultivation

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and preserves
  • Desserts and baking

Interesting Facts

  • Plumcots are about 50% plum and 50% apricot.
  • They were popularized through selective hybrid breeding programs.
