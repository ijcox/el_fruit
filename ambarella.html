Ambarella

Ambarella (Spondias dulcis), also known as June plum, golden apple, or hog plum, is a tropical fruit native to Polynesia and widely cultivated throughout Southeast Asia, the Caribbean, and tropical regions worldwide. It is known for its crisp texture and tangy-sweet flavor.

Description

Ambarella fruits are oval and typically measure 2 to 4 inches in length. The skin is green when unripe and turns yellow-golden when ripe. The flesh is firm and juicy, surrounding a fibrous, spiny pit. Its flavor ranges from tart when unripe to mildly sweet and tangy when fully mature.

Distribution and Habitat

Ambarella is commonly grown in:

  • Southeast Asia
  • Caribbean islands
  • Pacific Islands
  • Tropical Americas

It thrives in tropical climates with warm temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Cultivation

Ambarella trees are fast-growing and can reach heights of up to 60 feet. They prefer:

  • Full sunlight
  • Well-drained soils
  • Tropical climates

The tree is relatively hardy and produces abundant fruit, often within a few years of planting.

Uses

Ambarella is versatile and used in both sweet and savory preparations:

  • Eaten fresh (ripe or unripe with salt or spices)
  • Pickles and chutneys
  • Juices and smoothies
  • Salads and savory dishes

Nutritional Value

Ambarella is rich in:

  • Vitamin C
  • Dietary fiber
  • Antioxidants

It is often valued for its refreshing and hydrating qualities.

Interesting Facts

  • The fruit is often eaten unripe with chili and salt in many cultures.
  • Its fibrous pit can make it slightly difficult to eat cleanly.
  • It is sometimes called "hog plum," though it differs from other fruits with the same nickname.
