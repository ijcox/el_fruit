Egg Fruit

Egg fruit (Pouteria campechiana), also known as canistel, is a tropical fruit valued for its bright yellow flesh and unique texture resembling a cooked egg yolk.

Description

Egg fruit is oval or round with smooth yellow skin. The flesh is dense, dry, and rich, with a mildly sweet flavor often compared to pumpkin or sweet potato.

Distribution and Habitat

Egg fruit is native to:

  • Mexico
  • Central America

It is also cultivated in:

  • Caribbean
  • Southeast Asia
  • Florida (USA)

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Cultivation

Egg fruit trees prefer:

  • Warm climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun
  • Moderate watering

They are relatively hardy and can tolerate short dry periods.

Uses

Egg fruit is used for:

  • Fresh consumption
  • Smoothies and milkshakes
  • Desserts and custards
  • Baking

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin A
  • Vitamin C
  • Carbohydrates
  • Fiber

Interesting Facts

  • The texture is often described as similar to a hard-boiled egg yolk.
  • It is sometimes called “yellow sapote.”
  • It is best eaten fully ripe to avoid dryness.
