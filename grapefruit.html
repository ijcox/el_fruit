Grapefruit

Grapefruit is a large citrus fruit known for its bittersweet flavor, believed to be a natural hybrid between the sweet orange and pomelo.

Description

Grapefruits are round, pale yellow to pink fruits with juicy segmented flesh that can range from tart to sweet depending on the variety.

Distribution and Habitat

Grapefruit is widely grown in:

  • United States (Florida, Texas, California)
  • China
  • Israel
  • Mexico

It thrives in warm subtropical and tropical climates.

Cultivation

Grapefruit trees prefer:

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Warm climates
  • Moderate watering

They are sensitive to frost and require long warm growing seasons.

Uses

Grapefruit is used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Juices
  • Breakfast dishes
  • Salads and desserts

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Vitamin A
  • Fiber
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • Grapefruit was first documented in the Caribbean in the 18th century.
  • It can interact with certain medications.
  • Pink and red varieties tend to be sweeter than white ones.
