Genip

Genip (Melicoccus bijugatus), also known as Spanish lime or mamoncillo, is a tropical fruit native to northern South America and the Caribbean.

Description

Genip fruits grow in clusters and have a green, leathery skin. Inside is a gelatinous, translucent pulp surrounding a large seed, with a sweet-tart flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

Genip is native to:

  • Colombia
  • Venezuela
  • Caribbean islands

It is also cultivated in tropical regions of Central America and Florida.

Cultivation

Genip trees prefer:

  • Hot tropical climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun
  • Moderate rainfall

They are large, shade-providing trees commonly grown in home gardens.

Uses

Genip is used for:

  • Fresh eating (sucking the pulp from the seed)
  • Juices and drinks
  • Snacks and street food

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Calcium
  • Fiber
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • The fruit is typically eaten by biting open the shell and sucking the pulp.
  • It is very popular in Caribbean street markets.
  • The tree can grow very large and long-lived.
