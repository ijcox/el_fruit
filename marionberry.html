Marionberry

Marionberry is a type of blackberry developed in Oregon, known for its deep flavor and balanced sweetness and tartness.

Description

The fruit is dark purple-black, elongated, and very juicy, with a rich, complex berry flavor often used in desserts and jams.

Distribution and Habitat

  • United States (Oregon)
  • Pacific Northwest

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Trellised growth habit

Uses

  • Pies and desserts
  • Jams and preserves
  • Ice cream flavoring

Interesting Facts

  • Developed by crossing blackberry varieties in the 1950s.
  • Often called the “Cabernet of blackberries.”
