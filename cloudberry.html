Cloudberry

Cloudberry (Rubus chamaemorus) is a rare Arctic fruit known for its golden-orange color and tart, aromatic flavor. It grows in cold, northern wetland environments and is highly prized in Nordic cuisine.

Description

Cloudberries resemble small raspberries or blackberries but are amber to orange in color when ripe. They have a soft, juicy texture and a sweet-tart flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

Cloudberries are found in:

  • Norway
  • Sweden
  • Finland
  • Russia (Siberia)
  • Canada
  • Alaska (USA)

They grow in Arctic and sub-Arctic bogs, tundra, and peatlands.

Cultivation

Cloudberry plants prefer:

  • Cold climates
  • Acidic, boggy soil
  • High humidity
  • Low competition environments

They are difficult to cultivate commercially due to their specific habitat needs.

Uses

Cloudberries are used for:

  • Jams and preserves
  • Desserts and pastries
  • Liquors (such as Nordic cloudberry liqueur)
  • Traditional medicine in northern cultures

Nutritional Value

They contain:

  • Vitamin C (very high)
  • Vitamin A
  • Antioxidants
  • Fiber

Interesting Facts

  • Known as “Arctic gold” due to rarity and value.
  • Ripen unevenly, often taking weeks to fully mature.
  • Highly protected in some regions due to scarcity.
