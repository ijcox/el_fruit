Sloe

Sloe (Prunus spinosa) is a small wild plum known for its tart flavor and use in traditional liqueurs.

Description

The fruit is small, round, and deep purple-black with a bluish bloom and very astringent flesh.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Europe (native)
  • Western Asia
  • Hedgerows and wild thickets
  • Temperate climates

Cultivation

  • Cold temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Wild shrub (often not cultivated commercially)

Uses

  • Sloe gin production
  • Jellies and preserves
  • Traditional herbal remedies

Interesting Facts

  • Fruits are typically harvested after the first frost.
  • Extremely sour when eaten raw.
