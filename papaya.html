Papaya

Papaya (Carica papaya) is a soft, tropical fruit known for its sweet orange flesh and edible black seeds.

Description

The fruit is large and oval-shaped with smooth green-to-yellow skin and bright orange or pinkish flesh when ripe.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Central America (origin)
  • South America
  • South Asia
  • Tropical regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Fast-growing plant

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Smoothies and juices
  • Salads and desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Contains papain, an enzyme used to tenderize meat.
  • Both fruit and seeds are edible.
