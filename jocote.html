Jocote

Jocote (Spondias purpurea) is a tropical fruit native to Central America, known for its tangy flavor and vibrant color.

Description

The fruit is small and oval, turning from green to red, purple, or yellow when ripe. It has soft, juicy flesh surrounding a large seed.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Central America
  • Mexico
  • South America

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • Dry to moderate rainfall regions
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil

Uses

  • Fresh eating (often with salt or chili)
  • Jams and preserves
  • Pickled fruit

Interesting Facts

  • Common street fruit in Central America.
  • Taste varies from sweet to very tart depending on ripeness.
