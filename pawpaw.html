Pawpaw

Pawpaw (Asimina triloba) is a North American native fruit known for its tropical-like flavor and custard-like texture.

Description

The fruit is large, greenish-yellow when ripe, with soft creamy flesh that tastes like a mix of banana, mango, and melon.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Eastern United States
  • River valleys and woodlands
  • Temperate forests

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Partial shade when young
  • Rich, moist soil
  • Cold-hardy tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Desserts and ice cream
  • Native food ingredient

Interesting Facts

  • Largest edible fruit native to North America.
  • Pollinated by flies, not bees.
