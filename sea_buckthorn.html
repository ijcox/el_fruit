Sea Buckthorn

Sea buckthorn (Hippophae rhamnoides) is a hardy orange berry known for its intense tart flavor and high vitamin content.

Description

The fruit is small, bright orange-yellow berries that grow densely on thorny shrubs and are extremely rich in nutrients.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Europe
  • Asia
  • Himalayan regions
  • Coastal and cold mountainous areas

Cultivation

  • Cold and harsh climates
  • Full sun
  • Sandy or poor soil (nitrogen-fixing plant)
  • Drought and salt tolerant

Uses

  • Juices and syrups
  • Supplements and oils
  • Medicinal products

Interesting Facts

  • Extremely high in vitamin C.
  • Used in traditional Tibetan and Mongolian medicine.
