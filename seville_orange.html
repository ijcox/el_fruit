Seville Orange

Seville orange (Citrus aurantium) is a bitter citrus fruit widely used for marmalade, flavoring, and traditional medicine.

Description

The fruit is medium-sized with rough orange skin and very sour, bitter pulp with seeds inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Spain (Seville region)
  • Mediterranean region
  • Middle East
  • Subtropical climates

Cultivation

  • Warm subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Hardy citrus tree

Uses

  • Marmalade production
  • Flavoring liqueurs
  • Essential oils

Interesting Facts

  • Classic fruit used in traditional British marmalade.
  • Too bitter to eat fresh for most people.
