Japanese Persimmon

Japanese persimmon (Diospyros kaki) is a sweet, edible fruit native to East Asia and widely cultivated around the world.

Description

The fruit is round or slightly flattened, orange when ripe, with soft jelly-like flesh that can be sweet or mildly astringent depending on variety.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Japan
  • China
  • Korea
  • United States (cultivated)

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Moderate rainfall

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Dried fruit (hoshigaki)
  • Desserts and baking

Interesting Facts

  • Some varieties must fully ripen before eating.
  • Very popular in East Asian cuisine and culture.
