Umbu

Umbu (Spondias tuberosa) is a drought-resistant fruit native to Brazil, known for its juicy, tangy-sweet flavor and survival in arid regions.

Description

The fruit is small, round, and greenish-yellow with soft juicy pulp surrounding a large seed, growing on a low tree with a thick tuberous root system.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Brazil (Caatinga region)
  • Dry northeastern Brazil
  • Arid and semi-arid climates

Cultivation

  • Hot dry climates
  • Full sun
  • Poor rocky soil
  • Drought-tolerant tree

Uses

  • Juices and drinks (umbu juice)
  • Jams and sweets
  • Traditional Brazilian foods

Interesting Facts

  • The tree stores water in underground tubers.
  • Known as a “tree of survival” in dry regions.
