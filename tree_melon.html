Tree Melon

Tree melon is a name often used for certain papaya-like fruits that grow on trees and have sweet, melon-like flesh.

Description

The fruit is typically oval or elongated with smooth yellow-orange skin and soft, juicy orange or pink flesh inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Tropical South America
  • Central America
  • Southeast Asia
  • Tropical and subtropical regions

Cultivation

  • Warm tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Fast-growing fruiting tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Juices and smoothies
  • Desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Often confused with papaya due to similar appearance.
  • Flavor can resemble melon, peach, or banana depending on variety.
