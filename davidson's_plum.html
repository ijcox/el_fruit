Davidson's Plum

Davidson’s plum refers to several species in the genus Davidsonia, native to Australia. These fruits are known for their striking dark color and intensely tart flavor.

Description

Davidson’s plums are oval-shaped fruits with dark purple to nearly black skin and deep red flesh. They have a sharp, sour taste, making them more suitable for processed foods than raw consumption.

Distribution and Habitat

They are native to:

  • Eastern Australia (Queensland and New South Wales)

They grow in subtropical and tropical rainforests, often as understory trees.

Cultivation

Davidson’s plum trees prefer:

  • Subtropical to tropical climates
  • Rich, well-drained soil
  • Partial shade
  • Consistent moisture

They can be grown in orchards or as ornamental trees.

Uses

Due to their sour flavor, they are used for:

  • Jams and preserves
  • Sauces and chutneys
  • Juices and beverages
  • Desserts and flavoring

Nutritional Value

They are rich in:

  • Antioxidants
  • Vitamin C
  • Anthocyanins (pigments with health benefits)

Interesting Facts

  • They are considered a “bushfood” in Australia.
  • The fruit’s deep color makes it useful as a natural dye.
  • There are several species, including Davidsonia pruriens and Davidsonia jerseyana.
