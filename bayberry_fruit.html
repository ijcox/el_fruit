Bayberry

Bayberry (Myrica rubra) is a small, round fruit native to East Asia, especially China and Japan. It is known for its sweet-tart flavor and deep red to purple color when ripe.

Description

Bayberries are small, spherical fruits with a bumpy exterior surface made up of tiny fleshy segments. The flesh is juicy and soft, with a single central seed.

Distribution and Habitat

Bayberry grows in:

  • China
  • Japan
  • Korea
  • Subtropical parts of Southeast Asia

It thrives in warm, humid climates and acidic soils.

Cultivation

Bayberry trees prefer:

  • Mild subtropical climates
  • Well-drained, acidic soil
  • Partial to full sunlight

The trees are evergreen and moderately hardy once established.

Uses

Bayberry is used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Juices and wines
  • Jams and syrups
  • Traditional Chinese medicine

Nutritional Value

Bayberries contain:

  • Vitamin C
  • Antioxidants
  • Organic acids
  • Dietary fiber

Interesting Facts

  • Also known as “yangmei” in China.
  • The fruit is highly perishable after harvest.
  • Often eaten fresh or preserved in sugar or salt.
