Pomegranate

Pomegranate (Punica granatum) is a jewel-like fruit known for its bright red seeds (arils) and sweet-tart juice.

Description

The fruit has a thick reddish rind filled with clusters of edible seeds surrounded by juicy, flavorful pulp.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Middle East (origin)
  • India
  • Mediterranean region
  • California and Southwest Asia

Cultivation

  • Dry, warm climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Drought tolerant shrub/tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating (arils)
  • Juices and syrups
  • Culinary garnishes

Interesting Facts

  • Symbol of fertility and abundance in many cultures.
  • Each fruit can contain hundreds of seeds.
