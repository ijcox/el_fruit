Wax Apple

Wax apple (Syzygium samarangense) is a tropical fruit known for its bell shape, crisp texture, and refreshing watery sweetness.

Description

The fruit is bell-shaped with thin waxy skin in shades of red, pink, or green, and very juicy white spongy flesh.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Southeast Asia (origin)
  • Taiwan
  • Thailand
  • Tropical regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Hot humid climates
  • Full sun
  • Rich well-drained soil
  • Evergreen tropical tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Fruit salads
  • Juices and desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Also called “rose apple” in some regions.
  • Extremely high water content, very refreshing.
