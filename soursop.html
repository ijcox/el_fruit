Soursop

Soursop (Annona muricata) is a tropical fruit known for its creamy white flesh and strong sweet-tart flavor.

Description

The fruit is large, green, and covered with soft spines, containing fibrous white pulp with black seeds inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Central America (origin)
  • South America
  • Caribbean
  • Tropical regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Hot humid climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Evergreen tropical tree

Uses

  • Juices and smoothies
  • Desserts and ice cream
  • Traditional herbal uses

Interesting Facts

  • Also called guanábana in Spanish-speaking countries.
  • Flavor often described as strawberry-pineapple-citrus blend.
