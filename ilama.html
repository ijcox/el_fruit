Ilama

Ilama (Annona diversifolia) is a tropical fruit native to Central America, closely related to cherimoya and soursop.

Description

Ilama fruits are large and can have green or pink skin with soft, creamy flesh inside. The flavor is sweet and custard-like.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Mexico
  • Guatemala
  • El Salvador

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • Warm temperatures
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Desserts
  • Traditional fruit consumption

Interesting Facts

  • Considered a rare delicacy outside its native range.
  • Can have white, pink, or purplish flesh depending on variety.
