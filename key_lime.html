Key Lime

Key lime (Citrus aurantiifolia) is a small, aromatic citrus fruit known for its strong acidity and distinctive flavor.

Description

The fruit is small and round, green to yellow when ripe, with thin skin and very tart, fragrant juice.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Florida Keys (USA)
  • Mexico
  • Caribbean
  • Tropical regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Tropical to subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • Warm temperatures

Uses

  • Key lime pie
  • Drinks and cocktails
  • Flavoring and marinades

Interesting Facts

  • Smaller and more aromatic than Persian limes.
  • Famous for its use in Key lime pie.
