Nectarine

Nectarine is a smooth-skinned variety of peach known for its sweet, juicy flesh and rich aroma.

Description

The fruit is round with smooth, glossy skin ranging from yellow to red, and soft, fragrant flesh surrounding a central stone.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • United States
  • Europe
  • Worldwide cultivation

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Requires winter chill

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Desserts and pies
  • Jams and preserves

Interesting Facts

  • Nectarines are genetically very similar to peaches.
  • The smooth skin is due to a recessive gene.
