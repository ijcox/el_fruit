Bearberry

Bearberry (Arctostaphylos uva-ursi) is a small, low-growing shrub that produces tiny red berries. It is native to cold, northern regions and is more commonly known as a wild edible and medicinal plant than a commercial fruit crop.

Description

Bearberries are small, round, red fruits that grow in clusters on evergreen shrubs. They are firm, slightly mealy in texture, and contain several hard seeds. The plant spreads low along the ground, forming dense mats.

Distribution and Habitat

Bearberry is found in:

  • North America (Canada, Alaska, northern USA)
  • Northern Europe
  • Asia (cold regions)

It thrives in cold, dry, sandy, and rocky soils, often in alpine or tundra environments.

Cultivation

Bearberry prefers:

  • Cold climates
  • Acidic, well-drained soil
  • Full sun to partial shade

It grows slowly and is often used as ground cover rather than for fruit production.

Uses

Bearberry is used for:

  • Traditional herbal medicine
  • Wildlife food source
  • Occasional jams and preserves (after processing)

Nutritional Value

Bearberries contain:

  • Antioxidants
  • Tannins
  • Arbutin (used in herbal medicine)

Interesting Facts

  • The name “bearberry” comes from bears eating the fruit.
  • It is often used in traditional urinary tract remedies.
  • The plant is extremely hardy and survives in harsh climates.
