Ximenia

Ximenia is a small tropical fruit from the genus Ximenia, known for its tart flavor and orange to yellow skin when ripe.

Description

The fruit is oval-shaped with thin skin and a sour, sometimes slightly sweet pulp surrounding a large seed.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Africa (native)
  • Madagascar
  • Asia
  • Tropical and subtropical regions

Cultivation

  • Dry tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soils
  • Wild-growing shrub or small tree

Uses

  • Traditional medicine
  • Oil extraction from seeds
  • Occasional fresh consumption

Interesting Facts

  • Seeds produce oil used in cosmetics.
  • Highly drought tolerant plant.
