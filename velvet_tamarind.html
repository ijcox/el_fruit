Velvet Tamarind

Velvet tamarind (Dialium guineense) is a small African fruit known for its hard dark shell and tangy-sweet powdery pulp inside.

Description

The fruit is round with a rough, velvety black shell that cracks open to reveal dry brownish edible pulp surrounding a seed.

Distribution and Habitat

  • West Africa
  • Central Africa
  • Sub-Saharan tropical forests

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Forest or savanna environments
  • Wild and semi-cultivated tree

Uses

  • Snacking (raw pulp)
  • Traditional drinks
  • Medicinal uses

Interesting Facts

  • Not closely related to true tamarind despite the name.
  • The pulp is naturally dry and powdery rather than juicy.
