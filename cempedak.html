Cempedak

Cempedak (Artocarpus integer) is a tropical fruit closely related to jackfruit and breadfruit. It is native to Southeast Asia and is prized for its sweet aroma and soft, fibrous flesh.

Description

The fruit is elongated with a rough, green to yellow-brown rind. Inside, it contains golden-yellow arils that are soft, sweet, and highly fragrant when ripe.

Distribution and Habitat

Cempedak is grown in:

  • Malaysia
  • Indonesia
  • Thailand
  • Brunei
  • Southern Philippines

It thrives in hot, humid tropical rainforest climates.

Cultivation

Cempedak trees prefer:

  • High humidity
  • Deep, fertile soil
  • Warm tropical temperatures
  • Regular rainfall

The trees can grow quite large and produce seasonal heavy fruiting.

Uses

Cempedak is used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Fried fritters (popular street food)
  • Desserts
  • Processed sweets and jams

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Carbohydrates
  • Vitamin C
  • Potassium
  • Dietary fiber

Interesting Facts

  • The smell is very strong and often described as sweeter than jackfruit.
  • Fried cempedak is a popular snack in Southeast Asia.
  • The seeds are also edible when cooked.
