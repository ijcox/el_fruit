Sapodilla

Sapodilla (Manilkara zapota) is a tropical fruit known for its brown skin, grainy texture, and caramel-like sweetness.

Description

The fruit is round to oval with rough brown skin and soft tan flesh containing glossy black seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Mexico (origin)
  • Central America
  • India
  • Tropical regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Hot tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Evergreen tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Milkshakes and smoothies
  • Desserts

Interesting Facts

  • Flavor is often compared to brown sugar or pear.
  • The tree once supplied chicle, used in chewing gum.
