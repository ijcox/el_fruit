Araza

Araza (Eugenia stipitata) is a tropical fruit native to the western Amazon rainforest. It is known for its bright yellow color and intensely tart flavor, making it more commonly used in processed products rather than eaten fresh.

Description

Araza fruits are round to slightly flattened and typically measure 2 to 4 inches in diameter. The skin is thin and yellow when ripe, while the pulp is soft, juicy, and highly acidic. The fruit contains several small seeds embedded in the flesh.

Distribution and Habitat

Araza is native to the Amazon Basin, particularly in:

  • Peru
  • Brazil
  • Colombia
  • Ecuador

It grows in humid tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforest regions.

Cultivation

Araza trees are relatively small and bushy, making them suitable for cultivation in tropical orchards. They prefer:

  • Warm, humid climates
  • Well-drained soils
  • Partial to full sunlight

The trees are fast-growing and can begin producing fruit within a few years.

Uses

Due to its strong acidity, araza is rarely eaten raw. Instead, it is commonly used in:

  • Juices and smoothies
  • Jams and jellies
  • Ice creams and desserts
  • Flavorings for beverages

Nutritional Value

Araza is rich in:

  • Vitamin C
  • Antioxidants
  • Dietary fiber

Its high acidity contributes to its refreshing taste in processed forms.

Interesting Facts

  • Araza is sometimes called "Amazonian guava" due to its appearance and family relation.
  • It has significant potential in the food industry for flavoring products.
  • The fruit bruises easily, limiting its export as a fresh product.
