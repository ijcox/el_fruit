Tamarind

Tamarind (Tamarindus indica) is a tropical fruit known for its sticky pulp and distinctive sweet-sour flavor used widely in cooking.

Description

The fruit grows in long brown pods containing a sticky brown pulp surrounding hard seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Africa (origin)
  • India
  • Southeast Asia
  • Tropical and subtropical regions

Cultivation

  • Hot dry to tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Large long-living tree

Uses

  • Cooking and sauces
  • Beverages and chutneys
  • Traditional medicine

Interesting Facts

  • Key ingredient in Worcestershire sauce.
  • Both sweet and sour varieties exist.
