Kandis

Kandis refers to fruit from several Garcinia species, known for their sour flavor and use as a natural souring agent in Southeast Asian cuisine.

Description

The fruit is small to medium-sized, typically yellow or orange when ripe, with very sour pulp and a few large seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Malaysia
  • Indonesia
  • Thailand
  • Southeast Asia tropical forests

Cultivation

  • Tropical rainforest climates
  • High humidity
  • Shaded to semi-shaded environments
  • Moist, rich soil

Uses

  • Souring agent in cooking
  • Curries and sauces
  • Traditional medicine

Interesting Facts

  • Often used similarly to tamarind or lime in cooking.
  • Part of the Garcinia family, which includes mangosteen.
