Safety & contraindications

Baseline toxicity: generally low for culinary-use plants unless noted otherwise; verify species-specific limits. Avoid high intake in pregnancy or for young children unless supervised. Screen for interactions with anticoagulant, antihypertensive, sedative, or hypoglycemic medications.

Evidence & source

Tradition: Western culinary herbalism and regional folk practice. Evidence: Primarily traditional use; consult monographs and small clinical reports for details.

Preparation & dose

Infusion of 1–2 tsp dried aerial parts steeped 10–15 min; tincture 1:5 in 25–45% alcohol, 2–4 mL up to 3x/day; fresh poultice as indicated

Part used & harvest

Seeds or fruits when fully ripe and dry. Season: Late season once seed heads dry; finish drying under cover. Fresh aromatic leaves excel when used promptly; roots, barks, and seeds keep best once fully dried.

Primary uses

General culinary and folk support for gut comfort, light tonic effects, and bridging aromatic warmth into daily food and tea routines.

Growth & cultivation

Hardiness: many types manage in USDA 4–9 with winter mulch; prefers full sun to light shade and well-drained but moist soil; pots help control vigor and avoid spread. Use clean starts, avoid lookalikes, and monitor for invasive spread.

Flavor/aroma profile

Mild savory or bittersweet with earthy or marine notes; adapts to soups, sautéed greens, vinegars, and infusion blends for everyday tonics

Compliance/regulatory

Generally GRAS in culinary amounts; check country-specific limits for concentrated extracts and reference EMA/ESCOP/WHO monographs where available
