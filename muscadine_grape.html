Muscadine Grape

Muscadine grape (Vitis rotundifolia) is a native North American grape known for its thick skin, strong flavor, and heat tolerance.

Description

The fruit is larger than typical grapes, with thick bronze, green, or dark purple skin and sweet, aromatic pulp inside.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Southeastern United States
  • Warm, humid climates
  • Woodlands and river areas

Cultivation

  • Hot, humid climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained sandy soil
  • High disease resistance

Uses

  • Wine production
  • Jellies and jams
  • Fresh eating

Interesting Facts

  • One of the few grape species native to the U.S.
  • Extremely resistant to pests and diseases.
