Raspberry

Raspberry (Rubus idaeus and related species) is a soft aggregate berry prized for its bright flavor and delicate texture.

Description

The fruit is made of many tiny drupelets clustered together, usually red but also black, purple, yellow, or golden depending on variety.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Europe
  • Asia
  • North America
  • Temperate regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Cool to temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Rich, well-drained soil
  • Canes require pruning support

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and preserves
  • Desserts and beverages

Interesting Facts

  • Raspberries are not true berries botanically.
  • Picked fruit leaves a hollow center.
