Kiwi

Kiwi fruit (Actinidia deliciosa) is a small, fuzzy fruit known for its bright green flesh and sweet-tart flavor.

Description

The fruit is oval with brown fuzzy skin and vibrant green flesh containing tiny black edible seeds arranged in a circular pattern.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • New Zealand
  • Italy
  • Chile

Cultivation

  • Temperate climates
  • Vines requiring support structures
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Fruit salads
  • Desserts and smoothies

Interesting Facts

  • Originally called “Chinese gooseberry.”
  • New Zealand popularized the name “kiwi.”
