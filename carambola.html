Carambola (Star Fruit)

Carambola, commonly known as star fruit, is a tropical fruit named for its distinctive star-shaped cross-sections. It has a sweet-tart flavor and is widely eaten fresh or used as a garnish.

Description

The fruit is yellow-green with waxy skin and five prominent ridges. When sliced crosswise, it forms a perfect star shape. The flesh is juicy and crisp.

Distribution and Habitat

Carambola is grown in:

  • Malaysia
  • Philippines
  • India
  • Brazil
  • Thailand
  • United States (Florida and Hawaii)

It thrives in humid tropical and subtropical climates.

Cultivation

Star fruit trees prefer:

  • Full sun
  • High humidity
  • Well-drained soil
  • Protection from frost

They are fast-growing and can produce fruit multiple times a year.

Uses

Carambola is used for:

  • Fresh eating
  • Fruit salads
  • Juices and smoothies
  • Garnishes for dishes and drinks

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Fiber
  • Antioxidants
  • Low calories

Interesting Facts

  • The star shape appears only when sliced crosswise.
  • It is sometimes used in decorative food presentations.
  • Unripe fruit is more sour and crisp than ripe fruit.
