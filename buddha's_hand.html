Buddha's Hand

Buddha’s Hand (Citrus medica var. sarcodactylis) is a unique citrus fruit known for its finger-like segments and strong fragrance. It is primarily used for its aromatic zest rather than juice or pulp.

Description

The fruit resembles a yellow hand with elongated “fingers.” Unlike most citrus fruits, it contains little or no pulp or juice, consisting mostly of rind and pith.

Distribution and Habitat

Buddha’s Hand is grown in:

  • China
  • India
  • Japan
  • California (USA)

It thrives in warm subtropical and Mediterranean climates.

Cultivation

The plant prefers:

  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Mild winters (frost-sensitive)

It is often grown as an ornamental citrus tree due to its unusual appearance.

Uses

Buddha’s Hand is used for:

  • Flavoring dishes and drinks
  • Perfumes and aromatherapy
  • Decorative displays
  • Candied citrus peel

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Essential oils (limonene-rich)
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • It is considered a symbol of happiness and longevity in Asia.
  • It is often given as a gift during Lunar New Year.
  • The fragrance can last for weeks after harvest.
