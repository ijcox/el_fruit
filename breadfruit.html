Breadfruit

Breadfruit (Artocarpus altilis) is a large tropical fruit known for its starchy texture and versatility as a staple food in many island cultures.

Description

Breadfruit is round to oval, green with a rough, spiky surface. When cooked, its flesh becomes soft, starchy, and similar in texture to bread or potatoes.

Distribution and Habitat

Breadfruit is native to:

  • Polynesia
  • Melanesia
  • Micronesia

It is now widely grown in:

  • The Caribbean
  • Southeast Asia
  • Central America

Cultivation

Breadfruit trees prefer:

  • Tropical climates
  • High humidity
  • Well-drained fertile soil

The trees are fast-growing and can produce large yields annually.

Uses

Breadfruit is used for:

  • Boiling, roasting, and baking
  • Frying (as chips or fries)
  • Staple carbohydrate source
  • Traditional island dishes

Nutritional Value

Breadfruit contains:

  • Complex carbohydrates
  • Fiber
  • Vitamin C
  • Potassium

Interesting Facts

  • It was famously transported from the Pacific to the Caribbean in the 18th century.
  • It can replace rice, potatoes, or bread in many diets.
  • A single tree can produce hundreds of fruits per year.
