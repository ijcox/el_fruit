Ugli Fruit

Ugli fruit is a Jamaican citrus hybrid, a natural cross between grapefruit, orange, and tangerine, known for its rough wrinkled skin and sweet tangy flavor.

Description

The fruit is large and irregularly shaped with yellow-green wrinkled skin and juicy segmented flesh that is sweeter than grapefruit.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Jamaica (origin)
  • Caribbean
  • United States (Florida, California)
  • Subtropical citrus regions

Cultivation

  • Warm subtropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Citrus hybrid tree

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Juices
  • Fruit salads

Interesting Facts

  • The name “Ugli” comes from its rough appearance.
  • Despite its look, it is often very sweet and aromatic.
