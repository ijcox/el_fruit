Yellow Passion Fruit

Yellow passion fruit (Passiflora edulis f. flavicarpa) is a tropical vine fruit known for its highly aromatic, tangy-sweet pulp and strong fragrance.

Description

The fruit is round to oval with a thick bright yellow rind and a jelly-like interior filled with edible seeds surrounded by fragrant pulp.

Distribution and Habitat

  • South America (origin)
  • Brazil
  • Colombia
  • Tropical and subtropical regions worldwide

Cultivation

  • Warm tropical climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained fertile soil
  • Climbing vine plant requiring support

Uses

  • Juices and smoothies
  • Desserts and ice creams
  • Sauces and flavoring

Interesting Facts

  • More acidic but often more productive than purple passion fruit.
  • The aroma intensifies as the fruit ripens and wrinkles slightly.
