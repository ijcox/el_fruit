Langsat

Langsat (Lansium parasiticum) is a tropical fruit native to Southeast Asia, known for its sweet and slightly tart translucent flesh.

Description

The fruit grows in clusters and has a thin yellowish skin. Inside are segments of juicy, white flesh similar to garlic cloves.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Malaysia
  • Indonesia
  • Thailand
  • Philippines

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • High humidity
  • Well-drained soil
  • Partial shade to full sun

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Desserts
  • Traditional remedies

Interesting Facts

  • Closely related to duku and longkong varieties.
  • Seeds can be bitter and should be avoided.
