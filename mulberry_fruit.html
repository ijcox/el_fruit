Mulberry

Mulberry is a sweet, berry-like fruit that grows on Morus trees and is enjoyed fresh or dried in many parts of the world.

Description

The fruit resembles elongated blackberries and can be red, white, or deep purple-black when ripe, depending on the species.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Asia
  • Europe
  • North America
  • Temperate and subtropical regions

Cultivation

  • Full sun
  • Adaptable to many soils
  • Moderate watering
  • Fast-growing trees

Uses

  • Fresh eating
  • Jams and syrups
  • Dried fruit snacks
  • Wine production

Interesting Facts

  • White mulberries were historically used in silk production regions.
  • Birds love them, making harvesting tricky.
