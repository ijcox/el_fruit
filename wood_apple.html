Wood Apple

Wood apple (Limonia acidissima) is a hard-shelled tropical fruit known for its strong aroma and sticky, tangy pulp.

Description

The fruit has a very hard woody shell that must be cracked open to access the brown, paste-like aromatic pulp inside containing seeds.

Distribution and Habitat

  • India (native)
  • Sri Lanka
  • Southeast Asia
  • Dry tropical regions

Cultivation

  • Hot dry climates
  • Full sun
  • Poor, well-drained soils
  • Slow-growing hardy tree

Uses

  • Juices and sherbets
  • Traditional medicine
  • Chutneys and sweets

Interesting Facts

  • The shell is extremely hard—almost stone-like.
  • Strongly aromatic pulp is an acquired taste.
