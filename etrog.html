Etrog

Etrog is a citron fruit (Citrus medica) traditionally used in Jewish rituals during the festival of Sukkot. It is one of the oldest cultivated citrus fruits.

Description

Etrog fruits are large, yellow citrus fruits with thick, bumpy rinds. They are highly aromatic and contain very little pulp compared to other citrus fruits.

Distribution and Habitat

Etrog is grown in:

  • Israel
  • Italy
  • Morocco
  • India

It thrives in warm, Mediterranean-type climates.

Cultivation

Etrog trees prefer:

  • Mild, frost-free climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun
  • Moderate watering

The fruit is carefully cultivated to meet religious requirements, often requiring perfect shape and skin quality.

Uses

Etrog is used for:

  • Religious rituals (Sukkot ceremonies)
  • Traditional medicine
  • Preserves and marmalades (rarely)

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Essential oils
  • Antioxidants

Interesting Facts

  • Etrog must meet strict purity and shape standards for ritual use.
  • It is one of the four species used during Sukkot.
  • The fruit is often kept carefully for ceremonial purposes rather than eaten.
