Durian

Durian is a tropical fruit from trees in the genus Durio, famous for its large size, spiky shell, and extremely strong odor. It is often called the “king of fruits” in Southeast Asia.

Description

Durian fruits are large, oval, and covered in sharp spikes. Inside, the flesh is creamy and ranges from pale yellow to deep orange. The taste is rich and complex, often described as a mix of sweet, savory, and custard-like flavors.

Distribution and Habitat

Durian is primarily grown in:

  • Thailand
  • Malaysia
  • Indonesia
  • Philippines

It thrives in tropical rainforests with high humidity and rainfall.

Cultivation

Durian trees prefer:

  • Hot, humid climates
  • Deep, fertile soil
  • Regular rainfall
  • Low wind exposure

Trees can take several years to mature and produce fruit.

Uses

Durian is used for:

  • Fresh consumption
  • Desserts and sweets
  • Ice creams and pastries
  • Traditional dishes

Nutritional Value

Durian contains:

  • High calories
  • Vitamin C
  • Potassium
  • Healthy fats
  • Fiber

Interesting Facts

  • Its strong odor has led to bans in some public places like hotels and transportation.
  • Despite the smell, many people consider it delicious.
  • The fruit naturally falls when ripe, which can be dangerous due to its size and spikes.
