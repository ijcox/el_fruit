Noni

Noni (Morinda citrifolia) is a tropical fruit known for its strong odor and traditional medicinal uses across Polynesia and Southeast Asia.

Description

The fruit is oval, green turning pale yellow/white when ripe, with a soft, bumpy surface and pungent smell often compared to cheese or rancid fruit.

Distribution and Habitat

  • Polynesia
  • Southeast Asia
  • Australia
  • Tropical coastal regions

Cultivation

  • Tropical climates
  • Coastal sandy soils
  • Full sun
  • Drought tolerant plant

Uses

  • Traditional medicine
  • Juices and supplements
  • Herbal remedies

Interesting Facts

  • Often called “cheese fruit” due to its smell.
  • Highly valued in traditional Polynesian medicine.
