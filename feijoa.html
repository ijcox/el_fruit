Feijoa

Feijoa, also known as pineapple guava (Acca sellowiana), is a small green tropical fruit native to South America. It is valued for its fragrant aroma and sweet-tart flavor.

Description

Feijoa fruits are oval-shaped with dull green skin. The flesh is soft, grainy, and jelly-like near the center, with a flavor combining pineapple, guava, and mint.

Distribution and Habitat

Feijoa is native to:

  • Southern Brazil
  • Paraguay
  • Uruguay
  • Northern Argentina

It is now grown in:

  • New Zealand
  • Australia
  • California (USA)
  • Southern Europe

Cultivation

Feijoa trees prefer:

  • Mild subtropical climates
  • Well-drained soil
  • Full sun
  • Moderate watering

They are hardy and can tolerate light frost.

Uses

Feijoa is used for:

  • Fresh eating (often scooped with a spoon)
  • Jams and jellies
  • Desserts and baking
  • Juices and smoothies

Nutritional Value

It contains:

  • Vitamin C
  • Fiber
  • Antioxidants
  • Iodine (notably high compared to other fruits)

Interesting Facts

  • The flowers are also edible and visually striking.
  • The fruit is often eaten by cutting it in half and scooping out the flesh.
  • It is highly aromatic when ripe.
