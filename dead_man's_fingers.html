Dead Man’s Fingers

“Dead Man’s Fingers” can refer to a few different plants, but as a fruit it most commonly describes Decaisnea fargesii, also known as the blue sausage fruit. It is notable for its unusual shape and vivid blue color.

Description

The fruit grows in elongated, sausage-like pods with bright blue skin. Inside is a translucent, jelly-like pulp with small black seeds and a mildly sweet flavor.

Distribution and Habitat

This plant is native to:

  • China
  • Himalayan regions (Nepal, Bhutan)

It grows in mountainous forests and prefers cool, moist environments.

Cultivation

Dead Man’s Fingers plants prefer:

  • Cool temperate climates
  • Partial shade
  • Moist, well-drained soil
  • Sheltered conditions

They are often grown as ornamental plants due to their unusual fruits.

Uses

The fruit is used for:

  • Fresh consumption (mild taste)
  • Ornamental garden interest

It is not widely used commercially due to its subtle flavor.

Nutritional Value

Limited data is available, but the fruit contains:

  • Natural sugars
  • Water-rich pulp
  • Trace vitamins

Interesting Facts

  • The fruit’s appearance resembles fingers, giving it its name.
  • The bright blue color is rare among fruits.
  • It is sometimes called “blue bean” or “blue sausage fruit.”
