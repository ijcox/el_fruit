Ume

Ume (Prunus mume) is a Japanese and Chinese fruit similar to apricot, known for its sour taste and cultural importance in East Asia.

Description

The fruit is small and round with green to yellow skin and very sour flesh, usually not eaten raw but processed into other foods.

Distribution and Habitat

  • China (origin)
  • Japan
  • Korea
  • Temperate East Asia

Cultivation

  • Cool temperate climates
  • Full sun
  • Well-drained soil
  • Deciduous flowering tree

Uses

  • Umeboshi (pickled plums)
  • Umeshu (plum wine)
  • Syrups and preserves

Interesting Facts

  • Ume blossoms are celebrated in Japanese culture like cherry blossoms.
  • Closely related to apricots rather than true plums.
